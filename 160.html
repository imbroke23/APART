160. Maize Cobs. Inka. c. 1440- 1533 CE Sheet metal/repousse, metal alloys.

  • Technique and Form
    • Repousse technique
    • Hollow metal object
    • Life-sized
  • Function
    • May have been part of a garden in which full-sized metal sculptures of maize plants and other items were put in place alongside actual plans in the Qorikancha garden
    • May have been used to ensure a successful harvest
  • Context
    • Maize was the principal food source in the Andes
    • Maize was celebrated by having sculptures fashioned out of sheet metal
    • Black maize was common in Peru; oxidized silver reflects that
