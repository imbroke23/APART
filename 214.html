214. Moai on platform (ahu). Rapa Nui (Easter Island) c 1100-1600 CE Volcanic tuff figures on basalt base

  • Content
    • About 900 statues in all, mostly male
    • almost all facing inland
    • Erected on large platforms of stone mixed with ashes from cremations
    • the platforms are just as sacred as the statues on them
  • Function
    • images represent personalities deified after death OR….
    • commemorated as the first settler-kings
  • Form
    • Prominent foreheads
    • large broad noses
    • thin pouting lips
    • ears that stretch to the top of their heads
    • short thin arms falling straight down
    • hands crossed on lower abdomen below navel
    • chest & navels portrayed precisely
    • Topknots added to some statues
    • White coral placed in eyes to “open” them
