105. Self-Portrait. Elisabeth Louise Vigee Le Brun. 1790 CE Oil on canvas.

  • Form
    • Light Rococo touch in the coloring
    • Inspired by the portraits of Rubens
  • Context
    • Forty self-portraits exist, all highly idealized
    • The artist was 45 when this was painted, but she appears much younger
    • Painted in Rome during the French Revolution when the artist was exiled for political reasons
    • The artist was granted admission to the male-dominated French Royal Academy through influence of Marie Antoinette
    • The artist looks at the viewer as she paints a portrait of the French queen Marie Antoinette, who is rendered from memory since she was killed during the French Revolution
    • Subject in the painting looks admiringly upon the painter
