215 - ‘Ahu ‘ula (feather cape). Hawaiian (different one)

  • Content
    • Protected the wearer from harm
    • Cloak created by artistes who chanted the wearer’s ancestors to place their power on it.
  • Form
    • Coat made of thousands of bird feathers
    • 500,000 feathers; some from birds that only produce 7 usable feathers
    • red is considered a royal color in Polynesia
    • Coconut fiber used as a base; feathers tied to it
